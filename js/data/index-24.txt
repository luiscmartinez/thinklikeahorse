										<p align="justify"><strong><b>The Horse's Prayer</b></strong> <b><br>
										<br>
										- I'm only a horse, dear Master, but my heart is warm and true.<br>
										I'm ready to work my hardest, for the pleasure of pleasing you.<br>
										<br>
										Good corn, hay and water, are all that I wish to ask.<br>
										And a warm dry bed to rest on, when I've finished my daily task.<br>
										<br>
										Don't strike me in needless anger, if I'm slow to understand,<br>
										but encourage my drooping spirits with a gentle voice and hand.<br>
										<br>
										Finally, O my master! When my health and strength are gone -<br>
										When I'm getting old and feeble, and my long life's work is done -<br>
										<br>
										Don't sell me to cruel or strange owners, to be slaved to my last breath,<br>
										But grant me the untold blessing of a quick and painless death;<br>
										<br>
										That, as you have always found me a patient and loyal friend,<br>
										The years of my faithful service may be crowned by a peaceful end.<br>
										<br>
										I plead in the name of the Savior, Who cares when the sparrows fall.<br>
										Who was born in a lowly stable, and knows, and loves us all!<br>
										<br>
										<br>
										Your Faithful and loving friend,<br>
										<br>
										<strong>The Horse</strong><br>
										<br></b></p>
