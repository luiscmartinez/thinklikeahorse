										<p><strong>Carrot and Apple Cookies</strong><br>
										<br>
										1 cup sweet feed<br>
										2 cup bran<br>
										1 cup flax seed<br>
										4 large carrots, shredded<br>
										1 cup molasses<br>
										1/2 cup brown sugar<br>
										1 cup applesauce<br>
										<br>
										Mix molasses, brown sugar, carrots and applesauce in one bowl.<br>
										In another bowl mix the dry ingredients. Slowly combine the molasses<br>
										mixture with the dry ingredients. Add only enough molasses mixture to<br>
										form a thick dough, add more bran if necessary.<br>
										<br>
										Line cookie sheet with aluminum foil. Using a tablespoon, drop batter<br>
										onto cookie sheet and flatten slightly to form portions about the size of a<br>
										silver dollar. Bake at 300 degrees F for about 1 hour. Flip and bake for<br>
										an additional 45 minutes until they are dried out. Keep checking to make<br>
										sure they don't burn.<br></p>

										<p><strong>Whinny Bars</strong><br>
										<br>
										2 Cups Dry Oatmeal<br>
										3/4 Cup Grain<br>
										3 Cups Bran<br>
										1 Cup Molasses<br>
										<br>
										Mix Oats, Grain and Bran together in a bucket. Drizzle in Molasses while<br>
										mixing with you hands (you're looking for a consistency a little thinner<br>
										than Play-Doh). Place dollups (about half a handful) on a cookie sheet<br>
										and bake at 350 for ten minutes. These have a tendency to burn. They never get completely hard, but they store nicely.</p>

										<p><strong>Electrolyte Cookies<br></strong><br>
										2 cups oats (I use crimped oats or barley/mix)<br>
										3/4 cup grain (I use either LMF or barley)<br>
										3 cups bran<br>
										1 cup molasses<br>
										1 cup water (may vary depending upon altitude)<br>
										12-24 1-oz. scoops of powdered electrolytes (depends how strong you want them) Mix all the ingredients together. You should have a consistency a little thinner than play-doh. If it isn`t wet or sticky enough, add a little more water or molasses. Place large spoonfuls on a (greased) cookie sheet. Bake at 325 for 30-40 minutes. These have a tendancy to burn so keep an eye on them.</p>

										<p><strong>Cob Cookies</strong><br>
										<br>
										8 cups dry cob feed (corn,oats,barley mix)<br>
										3 cups ground carrots<br>
										1/2 cup corn oil<br>
										2 cups flour<br>
										2 cups molasses (use livestock grade from feed store - it's cheaper!)<br>
										<br>
										Mix all ingredients together in a large bowl until well mixed. Let stand<br>
										for at least an hour (so the grain can absorb some of the moisture).<br>
										Stir the mixture well. Drop by rounded teaspoonful on to a well oiled cookie sheet. Using your fingers and the teaspoon 'smoosh' the cookie into a round slightly flattened shape. Bake at 350 degrees for 12 to 18 minutes, depending on your oven. These will burn easily so be sure not to leave them in too long. Put the cookies on racks to cool then store them in a tight container. Makes between 6-9 dozen.</p>

										<p><strong>Apple Horse Cookies</strong><br>
										<br>
										1 c. sweet feed<br>
										2 - 3 c. wheat bran<br>
										1 c. flax seed<br>
										1 T. salt<br>
										4 large apples, shredded<br>
										1 c. molasses<br>
										1/2 c. brown sugar<br>
										1 c. applesauce<br>
										<br>
										Mix molasses, brown sugar, apples and applesauce in bowl. Mix dry<br>
										ingredients in a separate bowl. Gradually combine wet and dry<br>
										ingredients together, only using enough of the wet ingredients to make a<br>
										thick dough. Add more bran if necessary.<br>
										<br>
										Line cookie sheet with foil and spray with oil. Drop batter onto cookie<br>
										sheet in tablespoon amounts. Flatten with a fork. Bake slowly at 300ï¿½ F.<br>
										for 1 hour, turn cookies over and continue to bake for another 45<br>
										minutes until thoroughly dried. Reduce heat if cookies begin to brown<br>
										excessively or to burn.<br>
										<br>
										Store in covered container or zip-locked plastic bag and dole out as<br>
										special treats to your equine buddies.</p>

										<p><strong>Peppermint Bran Mash</strong></p>
										<p>Ingredients: 1 cup crushed candy canes or hard peppermint candies; 3 to 5 cups bran; 1 tablespoon salt; 1 carrot, diced; 1 carrot, diced; 1/2 cup molasses; 2 cups sweet feed. Dissolve crushed candy in 2 to 3 cups of boiling water and let water cool to warm. Then, mix all ingredients together except sweet feed in a feed bucket. Add more warm water to make it soupy, rather than crumbly. Cover with towel and let stand until cool. Right before serving, spread a thin layer of sweet feed over the top and sprinkle on some crushed candy to garnish</p><br>
